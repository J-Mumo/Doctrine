1. Being valuable: Capacity to solve problems, meet needs, provide solution and ability to answer questions. It is also measured using the index widespread desireability.
  In the kingdom, nothing has an independent intrinsic value. Value is derived wih respect to the ability to align with the will of God and His purposes.

2. Impactful: When your presence and your contribution makes people, things, places and situations better than you met them.
  It also measures the extent and quality of your contribution.

  Impact demands that you must look beyond yourself.

3. There is a price for an exceptional life.
  If you desire greatness, you must be willing to pay the price.
  The grace and the help of God can make achievements appear deceitfully effortless, it makes many believers to belittle the price for greatness.
  There is a price.
  - Mastery and competence
  - Faith
  - Courage
  - Patience
  - Discipline in the staying power and restraint.
  - Failing and not quiting
  - Sacrificing your desires to align with the will of God.
  - Warfare - contending against spirits and limiting beliefs.
  - Emotional strength to remain on course even when you are misunderstood.
  - Intentionally remaining humble in the midst of great results.
  - The discipline of consistency
  Most of the people who desire success cannot maintain it when it comes to them.
  The asignment of mentorship is not to take over the responsibility but subsidize the price.

## 7 anchor Tenets
1. Practice love and reverence towards God
  Proverbs 9:10 The fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom: and the knowledge of the holy is understanding.

  Job 28:28 And unto man he said, Behold, the fear of the Lord, that is wisdom; and to depart from evil is understanding.

2. Practice gratitude as a lifestyle; gratitude to God and to men
  1 Thessalonians 5:18 In every thing give thanks: for this is the will of God in Christ Jesus concerning you.

  Colossians 3:15 And let the peace of God rule in your hearts, to the which also ye are called in one body; and be ye thankful.

3. Practice humility as a lifestyle
  James 4:9-10 Be afflicted, and mourn, and weep: let your laughter be turned to mourning, and your joy to heaviness.
  Humble yourselves in the sight of the Lord, and he shall lift you up.

  1 Peter 5:5 Likewise, ye younger, submit yourselves unto the elder. Yea, all of you be subject one to another, and be clothed with humility: for God resisteth the proud, and giveth grace to the humble.

  Proverbs 22:4 By humility and the fear of the Lord are riches, and honour, and life.

  Proverbs 16:18 Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall.

4. Never stop learning and growing
  Proverbs 1:5 A wise man will hear, and will increase learning; and a man of understanding shall attain unto wise counsels:

  1 Corinthians 8:2 And if any man think that he knoweth any thing, he knoweth nothing yet as he ought to know.

5. Never outgrow the need to be guided.
  Psalms 25:9 The meek will he guide in judgment: and the meek will he teach his way.

  Proverbs 3:5-6 Trust in the Lord with all thine heart; and lean not unto thine own understanding.
  In all thy ways acknowledge him, and he shall direct thy paths.

  Acts 8:31 And he said, How can I, except some man should guide me

6. Practice honor as a lifestyle
  1 Samuel 2:30 Wherefore the Lord God of Israel saith, I said indeed that thy house, and the house of thy father, should walk before me for ever: but now the Lord saith, Be it far from me; for them that honour me I will honour, and they that despise me shall be lightly esteemed.

  Romans 13:7 Render therefore to all their dues: tribute to whom tribute is due; custom to whom custom; fear to whom fear; honour to whom honour.

  * Never fight a man you once honored publicly.

7. Walk in love
  Romans 12:10 Be kindly affectioned one to another with brotherly love; in honour preferring one another;
